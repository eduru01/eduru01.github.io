Payday Lender Location and Income Analysis

  • Type: Spatial Analysis • GIS • Urban Geography • Financial Exclusion
  • Technologies: ArcGIS Pro, Spatial Join, Local Moran's I, Attribute Query, Data Analysis
  • Course: Geospatial Information Systems
Investigated the relationship between payday loan lender locations and household family income levels based on census tracts and zip codes in Houston, Texas. Mapped payday lender locations as vector points and analyzed their distribution relative to traditional bank branches and median household income levels. Utilized spatial analysis methods including overlay spatial join, Local Moran's I clustering analysis, and attribute queries to identify patterns of financial exclusion and visualize structural inequality in financial institution access. The analysis examined whether payday lenders cluster in areas with lower income levels and in the absence of traditional banking services, revealing spatial patterns of financial exclusion across Houston neighborhoods.
