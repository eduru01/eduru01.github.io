Mapping Financial Inequality: Payday Lending in Houston, TX

  • Type: Spatial Analysis • GIS • Urban Geography • Financial Exclusion
  • Technologies: ArcGIS Pro (projection, geocoding, joins, Local Moran's I), ACS / Social Explorer (demographic datasets), Data Axle (business listings & NAICS code extraction), Excel (initial cleaning and formatting), ArcGIS Online (boundary data & visualization support)
  • Course: Geospatial Information Systems (GIS)
Mapping Financial Inequality examines how consumer lending sites, specifically payday and auto lenders, are distributed across Houston's Harris County and how their locations relate to neighborhood poverty and racial composition. Using geocoded business listings, census tract boundaries, and ACS demographic data, the project integrates spatial clustering methods (Local Moran's I) with demographic analysis to understand whether lenders target vulnerable communities or cluster along commercial corridors.

What I Did

  • Collected and cleaned consumer lending data from Data Axle (NAICS 522291) and geocoded 700+ locations using latitude and longitude.
  • Projected all datasets into NAD 1983 StatePlane Texas South Central (FIPS 4204) to ensure spatial consistency.
  • Clipped U.S. Census tracts to the Houston boundary and merged demographic attributes (poverty, race/ethnicity, population) from the ACS 5-year estimates via Social Explorer.
  • Conducted a spatial join to calculate the number of lenders per census tract and attach socioeconomic variables.
  • Ran a Local Moran's I cluster analysis to detect significant high–high, high–low, low–high, and low–low concentration patterns.
  • Compared these cluster types using summary statistics to understand how lending density relates to neighborhood characteristics.
  • Synthesized results into a spatial narrative explaining how consumer lending intersects with demographic and economic patterns in Houston.
