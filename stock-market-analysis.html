Economic Factors and the S&P 500

  • Type: Machine Learning • Financial Analytics • Predictive Modeling • Economic Analysis
  • Technologies: Python, pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, matplotlib, seaborn, FRED Data, Yahoo Finance API
  • Course: DATA 201 — Data Analytics & Machine Learning
Analyzing how macroeconomic indicators influence S&P 500 movements from 2006–2010 using logistic regression, random forest classification, and K-Means clustering. Built a complete ML workflow—from cleaning 11+ FRED datasets to feature engineering, exploratory visualizations, and market regime identification—to understand how economic conditions shape buy/sell trading signals during periods of financial stability and crisis.

What I Did

  • Collected and merged 11+ macroeconomic datasets (FRED) with S&P 500 data; cleaned and aligned time-series values (2006–2010).
  • Engineered features including 200-day moving averages, percent-change variables, and buy/sell target signals.
  • Conducted exploratory analysis—volatility trends, distribution plots, and correlation heatmaps—to understand macro–market relationships.
  • Built and evaluated Logistic Regression (87% accuracy, AUC 0.91) and Random Forest models; extracted feature importance to identify top predictors.
  • Applied PCA + K-Means to uncover four market regimes (bull, post-crisis, high-inflation, stressed/bear).
  • Synthesized results to interpret how unemployment, inflation, oil prices, and corporate profits influence market signals during financial turbulence.
